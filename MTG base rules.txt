MTG BASE RULES (STANDARD, 1v1)
Purpose: Base rules reference for building an MTG simulator.
Source: https://thegamerslodge.com/blogs/magic-the-gathering/a-beginners-guide-to-magic-the-gathering?srsltid=AfmBOoqRJMhEfOGvbloTarUH1t8GOeF7oIVYc2xWj4jKCN6fEs_FSR4U

------------------------------------------------------------
1) GAME OVERVIEW
------------------------------------------------------------
- Magic: The Gathering is a turn-based card game.
- In this simulator scope, match type is Standard, one-versus-one.
- Each player uses their own deck and attempts to win by game rules.

------------------------------------------------------------
2) STANDARD (SIMPLIFIED BASE FORMAT)
------------------------------------------------------------
- Deck size: minimum 60 cards.
- Sideboard: up to 15 cards (used between games in a match).
- Card pool: recent rotating sets (commonly around the last ~2 years).
- Copy limit: up to 4 copies of a card by English name, except basic lands.
- Starting life total: 20 life per player.

Note: Official legality, bans, and exact set rotation are maintained by Wizards.

------------------------------------------------------------
3) WIN CONDITIONS (CORE FOR SIMULATOR)
------------------------------------------------------------
A player loses if any of these happen:
- Life total is 0 or less.
- They attempt to draw from an empty library.
- They receive 10 or more poison counters.
- A card-specific alternate win/loss condition resolves.

If one player loses, the other player wins.

------------------------------------------------------------
4) GAME SETUP
------------------------------------------------------------
1. Each player presents a legal Standard deck.
2. Determine who plays first (random method).
3. Shuffle decks.
4. Each player draws 7 cards.
5. Mulligan process (London Mulligan):
   - A player may shuffle and redraw 7 cards repeatedly.
   - After keeping a hand, that player puts a number of cards from hand
     on the bottom of their library equal to mulligans taken.
6. Begin first turn.

------------------------------------------------------------
5) TURN STRUCTURE (PER TURN)
------------------------------------------------------------
Each turn follows this order:

1. Untap Step
   - Active player untaps tapped permanents they control.

2. Upkeep Step
   - "At upkeep" triggers and effects happen.

3. Draw Step
   - Active player draws 1 card.
   - (Player who goes first usually skips first-turn draw in official rules.)

4. Main Phase (Precombat)
   - Active player may play one land (if not already played this turn).
   - Active player may cast spells they can pay for.
   - Sorcery-speed restrictions apply.

5. Combat Phase
   a) Beginning of Combat
   b) Declare Attackers
   c) Declare Blockers
   d) Combat Damage
   e) End of Combat

6. Main Phase (Postcombat)
   - Active player may cast more spells.
   - No second land play unless an effect allows it.

7. End Step / Cleanup
   - End-step triggers resolve.
   - If active player has more than 7 cards in hand during cleanup,
     discard down to 7.
   - Damage marked on creatures is removed in cleanup.

------------------------------------------------------------
6) CARD TYPES (BASE SIMULATOR SUPPORT)
------------------------------------------------------------
- Land: primary mana source.
- Creature: can attack/block; has power/toughness and abilities.
- Instant: can generally be cast at any time player has priority.
- Sorcery: cast only during controller main phase when stack is empty.
- Artifact: nonland permanent with ongoing/static/activated effects.
- Enchantment: nonland permanent with ongoing/static/triggered effects.
- Planeswalker: permanent with loyalty abilities (typically once each turn).

------------------------------------------------------------
7) MANA AND CASTING (BASE LOGIC)
------------------------------------------------------------
- Mana is usually produced by lands and some other cards.
- To cast a spell:
  1) Move spell to stack.
  2) Choose modes/targets and pay all costs.
  3) Players may respond while spell is on stack.
  4) If not countered and all legal checks pass, spell resolves.
- Colored mana requirements must be satisfied exactly.
- Generic mana can be paid with any mana type.

------------------------------------------------------------
8) COMBAT (SIMPLIFIED RESOLUTION)
------------------------------------------------------------
- Creatures attack opposing player (or planeswalker if present).
- Defending player assigns blockers from untapped eligible creatures.
- Blocked/unblocked state determines damage assignment.
- Combat damage is dealt simultaneously unless abilities modify timing
  (example: first strike, double strike).
- Unblocked combat damage to player reduces life total.

------------------------------------------------------------
9) COMMON KEYWORDS (MINIMUM SET TO MODEL EARLY)
------------------------------------------------------------
- Flying: can only be blocked by flying/reach creatures.
- Reach: can block creatures with flying.
- Trample: excess combat damage can hit defending player/planeswalker.
- Haste: creature can attack/use tap abilities the turn it enters.
- Vigilance: attacking does not tap this creature.
- Lifelink: damage dealt causes controller to gain that much life.
- Deathtouch: any non-zero damage from source is lethal to creature.
- First Strike: deals combat damage in first strike step.
- Double Strike: deals damage in first strike and regular steps.

------------------------------------------------------------
10) STATE TRACKING FOR SIMULATOR
------------------------------------------------------------
Track at minimum per player:
- Life total
- Library size/order
- Hand
- Battlefield permanents
- Graveyard
- Exile
- Poison counters
- Land plays used this turn

Track at game level:
- Active player / turn number
- Current phase/step
- Stack
- Priority holder
- Trigger queue
- Win/loss flags
